About Me

I am a political scientist and scholar of public policy who recently defended my dissertation in the Department of Political Science at Texas A&M University. In Fall 2017, I will begin as an Assistant Professor in the School of Public Administration at Florida Atlantic University.

My work stands at the intersection between empirical political science and normative political theory, with an emphasis on environmental policy. My major research focus is on how political and administrative variables shape the implementation of environmental legislation, specifically the United States Safe Drinking Water Act. I have additional interests in public management, democratic theory, urban policy, and environmental justice. My dissertation investigates the implications of the privatization of US water utilities for democracy from an empirical and normative perspective.
